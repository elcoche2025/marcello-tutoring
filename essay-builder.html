After
reading about Apollo 11, I think...
Think about this...

This sentence starts your whole essay! You need to tell the reader what you learned about and what you think about it.

  • What was Apollo 11? (A mission to the Moon!)
  • What is your opinion about it? Was it important? Amazing? Exciting?
  • Remember: start with what you read, then share what you think!
1
After reading about Apollo 11, I think the Moon mission was very important.
Simple and clear - a great start!
2
After reading about Apollo 11, I think it was one of the most amazing things that ever happened.
Shows a strong opinion!
3
After learning about the Apollo 11 Moon mission, I think it was one of the most important events in history.
Directly answers the essay question!
I believe this because
Tell the reader WHY you think this
Think about this...

Now you need to explain WHY you have that opinion. Give a quick reason - you'll explain more in the body!

  • Why was the Moon mission so important or amazing?
  • Had anyone ever done this before?
  • What makes it special compared to other things that happened?
1
I believe this because no one had ever walked on the Moon before.
Simple and true - it was the first time ever!
2
I believe this because the astronauts did something really brave and amazing that changed the world.
Shows how big of a deal it was!
3
I believe this because it showed that people can do incredible things when they work together.
Connects to a bigger idea - teamwork!

First,
What is your first reason? Think about what the astronauts did.

This is your FIRST reason why you think Apollo 11 was important. Think about the astronauts and what they did!

  • What did the astronauts have to do? (Fly through space for 3 days!)
  • Was it dangerous? Why? (Their equipment was fragile!)
  • How would you describe the astronauts? (Brave? Courageous? Amazing?)

1

2

Uses the connecting word "because"!
3
First, the astronauts were very brave because their equipment was fragile and the mission was dangerous.
Uses "because" AND the vocabulary word "fragile"!

When
Think about what happened and what it meant for people.
Think about this...

This sentence starts with "When" - that means you'll describe a MOMENT and what happened because of it. Think about the most exciting moments of the mission!

  • What happened when Neil Armstrong stepped on the Moon?
  • How did people on Earth feel when they watched on TV?
  • What happened when the rocket launched?
  • Remember: "When [something happened], [something else happened]."
1
When Neil Armstrong took his first step on the Moon, the whole world watched and cheered.
Describes an amazing moment!
2
When the astronauts landed on the Moon, it showed that people can do incredible things.
Connects the event to a bigger meaning!
3
When the rocket launched into space, millions of people around the world held their breath.
Paints a picture of how exciting it was!
Also,
What else makes this important?
Think about this...

This is your chance to add one more cool fact or reason! Think about what lasted AFTER the mission ended.

  • Did you know the astronauts' footprints are STILL on the Moon? (There's no wind to blow them away!)
  • What did scientists learn from the Moon rocks they brought back?
  • Why do people still talk about Apollo 11 today?
  • Try using the vocabulary word "permanent"!
1
Also, the astronauts' footprints are still on the Moon today because they are permanent.
Uses "because" AND the vocabulary word "permanent"!
2
Also, the astronauts brought back Moon rocks so scientists could learn new things about space.
Shows another reason the mission mattered!
3
Also, people still remember Apollo 11 today because it was such an amazing achievement.
Uses "because" and the word "achievement"!
Conclusion

Wrap it up!

The conclusion reminds the reader what you think and why it matters. Try using although - it's a powerful connecting word!

Although
the Moon mission happened long ago...
Think about this...

"Although" is a special word that means "even though." It shows a contrast - two things that seem different but are both true!

  • Apollo 11 happened in 1969 - that's a long time ago. BUT does it still matter today?
  • "Although" pattern: "Although [one thing is true], [something different is ALSO true]."
  • Example: "Although it's raining, I'm still happy." See how the two parts contrast?
1
Although the Moon mission happened a long time ago, it is still important today.
Simple and clear contrast - great conclusion starter!
2
Although Apollo 11 happened in 1969, people still remember and celebrate it.
Shows that it has lasting impact!
3
Although the mission was dangerous and the equipment was fragile, the astronauts succeeded and made history.
Uses "fragile" AND shows contrast - powerful!
I think ... because
Restate your opinion one more time
Think about this...

This is your LAST sentence! Restate your opinion one final time. Use "because" to remind the reader of your strongest reason.

  • What is your opinion again? (Apollo 11 was important/amazing!)
  • What is the BIGGEST reason you feel this way?
  • What would you want the reader to remember most?
  • Try using the word "achievement" - it means something great that was accomplished!
1
I think Apollo 11 was one of the greatest achievements because it showed that people can do amazing things.
Strong ending with "achievement" and "because"!
2
I think everyone should learn about Apollo 11 because it proves that anything is possible when people work together.
Ends with an inspiring message!
3
I think the Apollo 11 mission will always be remembered because it was truly one giant leap for mankind.
Uses Neil Armstrong's famous words - great way to end!
