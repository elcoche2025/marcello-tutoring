Coherence Focus (from research)

Per Ábalos et al. (2025) + Sousa: narrative coherence rests on (a) Purpose relations ("so that what?"), (b) character mental states, and (c) a running paragraph gist that each new sentence checks against. All three are scaffolded in this session.

Phase 5: Write — Sentence by Sentence

22 mins 0:23 – 0:45 TAB 4

Goal: Write 2 narrative sentences (stretch: 3) using kernel + WHY + FELT, with coherence checks.

The Protocol per sentence

1
TELL ME (Oral, 1–2 min)

"Tell me the [beginning / big moment / ending]." Tutor types verbatim. Use Purpose Prompts to surface WHY and FELT.

2
FIND THE KERNEL (1 min)

"Find ONE short sentence hiding in your words — Subject + Verb (+ object)."

3
EXPAND with WHY + FELT (3 min)

Two cards: Purpose card (because/so/when) and Feeling card (with feeling-word bank). Auto-assembled into one sentence Marcello can edit.

4
3-QUESTION COHERENCE CHECK (1 min)

Marcello taps each box: Topic (matches gist anchor?) · Purpose (told WHY?) · Felt (reader can tell?). Read it back with speech.

Setting the Gist Anchor (start of Tab 4)

Before he writes ANY sentence, you set a single line at the top: "This paragraph is about ___'s first morning in America." The app pre-fills from his plan, but you can edit it.

Why this matters: Marcello's tangents are sentence-level gists never combining into a paragraph-level gist. The anchor externalizes that gist. Every sentence is checked against it.

Sentence 1: Set the Scene

7 min · 0:23 – 0:30

Prompt: "Tell me the BEGINNING. Where is your character? What are they doing?"

Kernel hint: "Like: Maria was on a ship." → Expand: WHEN/WHERE + initial FEELING.

Sentence 2: The Big Moment

8 min · 0:30 – 0:38

Prompt: "Tell me what your character SEES or DOES. The big moment in the story."

This is where Purpose relations matter most. Push for WHY this moment matters to the character.

Sentence 3: The Ending If Time

7 min · 0:38 – 0:45

Prompt: "Tell me how it ends. What does the character do next? How do they feel?"

Differentiation & Troubleshooting
If he goes off on a tangent during dictation:

Don't interrupt — TRANSCRIBE everything. Then point to the gist anchor and ask "Which of these stays on the path?" He selects. You celebrate the selection.

If he resists the FEELING card:

Mental-state language IS coherence (per Ábalos et al.). Use the feeling word bank — make it a tap-and-add, not a generate-from-scratch.

If kernel finding is hard:

Bracket his dictation aloud: "I hear lots of stories in here — listen: [first part]. THAT could be your kernel. Try it." Don't pick it for him; narrow the field.

If 2 sentences take the full 22 min:

Stop at 2. Quality > quantity. Two coherent sentences with WHY and FELT beat three event-list sentences. Move to Wrap-up.

If a coherence check fails:

Don't just say "you didn't tell why." Tap "Edit sentence" and add it together. The check is a prompt to revise, not a grade.
