Irregular Words (3 min)

Flashcard-style. Target "first-letter guessing" habit.

through league foreign brought straight knowledge
"Let's practice some tricky words! Remember, look at the WHOLE word — don't just guess from the first letters. Ready?" If correct: "Nice! You looked at the whole word!" If incorrect: App shows color-coded parts + phonetic guide. Cover-Write-Check for missed words.

Think vs Things (30 sec)

Diagnostic — can he distinguish these when focused?

  • THINK = what your brain does (no G, no S)
  • THINGS = stuff, objects (ends in G-S)
"Before we read, let's look at two words that sound similar. [Point to THINK] This is 'think' — T-H-I-N-K. What your brain does. [Point to THINGS] This is 'things' — T-H-I-N-G-S. Stuff! See the G and S at the end?"
If he gets all 3 quiz questions correct here but still writes "think" for "things" during essay work, it's a motor/spelling habit, not a knowledge gap. Track this.

Prosody Passage (4 min)

~100-word passage about recess. Rate prosody on 4 dimensions (1-4).

"Now let's read a short passage. This one is about... RECESS! Try to read it like you're telling your friend an exciting story. Make it sound interesting, not like a robot. Ready? Go!"
Coaching prompts
  • If monotone: "Try that sentence again — pretend you're really excited about it!"
  • If word-by-word: "Try reading it in chunks, like how you talk."
  • Target: expression 3/4, smoothness 3/4

Phase 3 • 0:10 - 0:20
Stars and a Wish
10 minutes • First revision experience
Tab 2
Goal

First experience with essay revision. Must be psychologically safe.

Critical Framing
"Real writers don't just write once — they revise! I read your sentences, and I found some AMAZING things. For each sentence, I'll show you the STARS — things you did great. And then sometimes I'll have a WISH — one thing we could make even better. Ready to see your stars?"
Sentence 1 — Introduction
"I think that exploring space is one of the most important things to do."
⭐ You stated your opinion clearly — I know exactly what you think!
⭐ You used 'one of the most important' — strong opinion words!
No wish — pure celebration!
Sentence 2 — Body Reason 1
"I believe this because they make new amazing things that help the astronauts discover amazing things on earth and in space too."
⭐ You used 'because' to connect your reason!
⭐ You explained both Earth AND space — two places!
💡 Wish: Be more specific — what KINDS of things?
"I believe this because scientists _______ that help astronauts _______."
If he fills in / keeps as is

If fills in: "Now look — you made it more specific! That's revision!"

If keeps: "That's okay! You can always keep your original."

Sentence 3 — Body Reason 2
"First, I think that scientists love learning space. They can discover more things if they want, so they can go back to space another time, in a new year."
⭐ You used 'First' to organize your essay — nice transition word!
💡 Wish: Name what scientists actually discover
"First, scientists can _______ when they explore space."
Sentence 4 — The "When" Sentence
"When the astronauts got new things from space is cool because things from space are fun"
⭐ You tried 'When' — that's brave! Subordinating conjunctions are hard!
💡 Wish: Fix the grammar. 'When' sentences need TWO parts.
"When astronauts _______ from space, it is _______ because _______."
"I love that you tried 'When' — that takes guts! Here's the thing about 'When' sentences: they need two parts. 'WHEN something happens, THEN something else.' Let me show you a frame that makes it work."
Never say it was "wrong." Say: "When sentences have a special pattern. Let's use the pattern!"
After all 4: "Amazing work! You just did what real writers do — you revised! Now let's finish your essay." → Tap Tab 3

Sentence 5: "Also" (2 min)

"Also, exploring space helps us _______ because _______."
learn discover understand protect build create
"Your essay has an intro and two reasons. Let's add one more detail! Step 1: SAY IT. Say a sentence that starts with 'Also' out loud. Step 2: TYPE IT. Fill in the blanks. Step 3: CHECK IT. [Tap Read Back] Does that sound right?"

Sentence 6: "Although" (4 min) ⚠️ Hardest

"Although _______ is _______, I still think space exploration is important because _______."
expensive dangerous hard scary risky far away
"Now the conclusion! We're going to use 'Although.' Remember, 'Although' means 'even though' — you say something that's true, BUT then you say why your opinion is still right. Step 1: SAY IT. 'Although something is hard or expensive...' Think about what someone who DISAGREES might say. Then say 'but I STILL think it's important because...' Step 2: TYPE IT. The frame helps you keep the grammar right. Step 3: CHECK IT. [Read Back] Does that sound like a real sentence?"
This is the hardest sentence. Use the word bank heavily. "Although" requires acknowledging a counterpoint.

Sentence 7: Final "I think" (2 min)

"I think everyone should _______ because _______."
care about support learn about explore
"Last sentence! Wrap it up — restate your big opinion one more time."

Reference
Differentiation + If Running Behind
Quick reference for adjustments

If Struggling with Stars & Wish

  • Spend more time on celebration (stars)
  • Reduce wishes to just Sentence 4 only
  • Use "keep as is" button — never force revision
  • If upset: "These make your essay BETTER. Real authors get feedback on every book!"

If Struggling with Sentence Frames

  • Read the frame aloud together first
  • Let him dictate; you type, then he copies
  • Use word bank chips exclusively (tap, don't type)
  • Reduce to 2 frames instead of 3

If Excelling

  • Encourage writing beyond the frame
  • "Instead of 'cool,' what's a stronger word?"
  • Try "Although" without the frame
  • Write 2-3 sentences in Essay 5

If Running Behind

  • At 0:20: Not done Stars & Wish → Show Sent 1 & 4 only
  • At 0:30: Not done Finish Essay → Do "Although" only
  • At 0:40: Not done planner → Skip Essay 5, go to Dashboard
Priority: Stars/Wish > Finish Essay > Planning > Essay 5 Writing

If "think/things" Appears During Writing

  • Point to the reference at top of writing area
  • "Which one do you need — the brain one or the stuff one?"
  • Track: correct in quiz but wrong in writing?

If STC Feels Awkward

  • Model it first: you say, type, read back
  • For "Say" step, accept pointing to frame and reading it
  • "Check" step: if speechSynthesis sounds robotic, you read instead
