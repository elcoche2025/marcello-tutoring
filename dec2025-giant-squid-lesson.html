Activity 2: Finding Text Evidence

Learning Standard: RI.3.1

Instructions: Read the passage below carefully. Then answer the questions using evidence (exact words or ideas) from the text.
From "Giant Squid: Searching for a Sea Monster":

"Long, long ago, sailors told scary stories about the ocean. They said a huge monster lived deep in the water. The monster had long, twisting arms. It could grab sailors right off their ships! The sailors called this creature the kraken. They said it was as big as an island. Its long arms could wrap around a whole ship and pull it down into the water."
💡 Remember: When you answer, use words from the text to prove your answer! Start with phrases like "The text says..." or "According to the passage..."

1 What did sailors call the sea monster they talked about?

2 According to the text, how big did sailors say the kraken was?

3 What could the monster's arms do, according to the sailors' stories?

Activity 5: Meet the Squid Hunter

Learning Standards: RI.3.1, RI.3.2

Instructions: Read about Clyde Roper, then answer the questions using text evidence.
Meet the Squid Hunter:

"A boy named Clyde Roper loved the ocean. When he was little, he would follow tiny snails along the beach. He watched them make trails in the sand.

Clyde didn't know it yet, but those snails were his first clue. Snails and squids are related! They're both soft-bodied animals called mollusks.

When Clyde grew up, he went to college. He studied all kinds of squids and their relatives. There are octopuses, cuttlefishes, and even a creature called the chambered nautilus."

1 What did Clyde Roper do as a child that showed his love for ocean life?

2 How are snails and squids related, according to the text?

3 What are THREE animals that are related to squids?

4 What does this section tell us about how scientists become interested in their work?
