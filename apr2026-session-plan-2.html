Primary Focus

Revise Apr 27's circular paragraph into a coherent journal entry with per-sentence territory + paragraph-level "What's NEW?" check. Plus 8-min CAPE warm-up using a real released passage.

The Apr 27 finding (the reason we're here)

"He stayed on the path — but the path was a circle, not a line."

Every sentence passed our 3-question coherence check, but the paragraph repeated the same event 4 times. Sentence-level coherence ≠ paragraph-level coherence. Today we're shipping the four toolkit additions specified in last week's session_notes.

Charlie's Apr 27 paragraph (4 sentences)

  1. Charlie went to NY with his dad and mother because they had never seen the Statue of Liberty before...
  2. A long time ago, Charlie and his parents sailed from Italy to NY to see the Statue of Liberty...
  3. Charlie and his parents felt excited when they finally got off the boat...
  4. They felt joyful as they went to the Statue of Liberty to take pictures...
Repeats: "Statue of Liberty" ×5 · "happy/joyful" ×4 · "first time" ×3 · "Italy" ×3

Toolkit shipping today (the 4 from last notes)

1. Per-sentence territory cards NEW Each slot says what it MUST cover and what it MUST NOT yet. S1 = WHO+WHERE+WHEN no feeling. S2 = ONE thing + ONE feeling. S3 = NEXT + DIFFERENT feeling.
2. "What's NEW?" 4th coherence check NEW After each sentence, Marcello checks: "Did I tell something the reader didn't know yet?" Auto-flagged if the sentence repeats nouns/words from prior sentences.
3. Varied-feeling enforcement NEW Feeling-word bank greys out used words at paragraph level. Happy/joyful/excited shown as "used a lot Monday — try the others first."
4. Single-sentence cap NEW Soft warning if "Combined sentence" textarea contains 2+ sentences. Apr 27's freeform textarea was the proximate cause of territory collapse.
5. Sensory card alt on S2 NEW (optional) SEE / HEAR / FEEL prompt as alternative to WHY card on the "Big Moment" sentence. Specificity > causation for narrative middles.
6. Pronoun lock NEW Pick gender at planning step → lock pronouns. First-person "I" voice in journal entry inherently solves the his/her slip.

Session Plan (50 min)

2 min

Phase 1 — Greeting + frame today

"Two big things today: (1) we're going to look at one of the actual reading passages from the test you take next week, and (2) we're going to rewrite Monday's story as Charlie's journal entry. Same character, but he's going to sound like a real kid in a real diary this time."

Don't say "your story was repetitive" — frame it as "we're going to make it tell more."

8 min

Phase 2 — Tab 1: CAPE Warm-Up 📖

Step 1 (1 min) — Tool rehearsal. Show Marcello the two test buttons: 🟨 Highlighter and ✗ Answer Eliminator. Tap each. Brief explainer: "Highlight the sentence in the passage that ANSWERS the question. X out 1-2 wrong choices before picking."

Step 2 (3 min) — Read the passage. Marcello reads silently, then aloud (don't tap "Read aloud" — no TTS accommodation). Note prosody informally. Tap "I read it."

Step 3 (3 min) — Two real EBSR items.

  • Q1 (RL.3.4 vocabulary): "What does cross mean in paragraph 7?" Correct: C (upset) + Part B: D ("They didn't hurry now"). Teach: both parts must be right for credit.
  • Q2 (RL.3.2 moral): "What is the moral?" Correct: D (be happy with what you have) + Part B: B (the Old Mother West Wind line). Teach: for "moral" questions, Part B is usually the sentence that DIRECTLY says the lesson.

Step 4 (1 min) — Show Q19 prompt. "On the test, after a story like this, you'll see a question that looks like this — write a character's journal entry. Notice anything? (Pause for him to spot the connection.) Yeah — that's exactly what we're doing today with Charlie."

7 min

Phase 3 — Tab 2: Look Back at Monday 🔍

Step 1 (2 min) — Listen to Monday's paragraph. Tap "Read whole paragraph aloud" (TTS is fine here — it's HIS paragraph being read back to him, not test prep). Mark "I listened."

Step 2 (3 min) — Tag each sentence. "Which one tells us the MOST? Which ones just RESTATE?" Expected: he stars S1 (carries WHO+WHERE+WHEN+feeling) and tags S2-S4 as restate. The repeat counts panel above is the receipts.

Step 3 (2 min) — Lock pronouns. "Charlie can be a boy or a girl — you pick. Once we pick, we lock it. (Pick.) OK, so when we write OUTSIDE the diary, we say [he/she]. But INSIDE the diary, Charlie says I. Today is the diary, so it's all I."

⚠️ Watch for: he might want to defend Monday's paragraph. Don't argue — let the receipts (5× Statue of Liberty) speak. Then say "today we're going to make it tell MORE."

6 min

Phase 4 — Tab 3: Plan the Journal Entry 📓

Step 1 (1 min) — Read the Q19 prompt aloud. "This is what the test asks. Today we're doing the same thing for Charlie."

Step 2 (5 min) — Plan 4 sentences with territory cards. Each sentence has a job AND a forbidden:

  • S1 — WHO+WHERE+WHEN. Where IS Charlie tonight? When? NO big moment, NO feeling.
  • S2 — ONE thing seen/done + ONE feeling. Pick ONE. Add ONE feeling that is NOT happy/joyful.
  • S3 — What happened NEXT + DIFFERENT feeling. A new event, a different feeling word.
  • S4 — Closing thought (stretch). "What does Charlie want to remember?"

If he tries to put everything in S1, point at the territory card. "S1's job is just where + when. The big moment is S2's job. Save it."

22 min

Phase 5 — Tab 4: Write Charlie's Journal Entry ✍️

Per sentence (~7 min each for S1+S2, ~5 min for S3, optional S4):

  1. Tell Me — start the talk timer; he says the sentence aloud first. Use Purpose Prompts (now includes "Stakes" — pushes past tautological because).
  2. Find Kernel — extract the subject + verb. First-person: "I am in...", "I saw...", "Then we..."
  3. Expand — add WHY (or sensory on S2). Add FEELING (skipped on S1 by territory). Watch the feeling bank greying. If happy/joyful/excited tempts him, point at the warning chip and say "you used those a lot Monday — try one of these."
  4. Coherence Check (4 questions) — Topic / Purpose / Felt / NEW. The NEW check auto-detects repeats and flags them. If flagged, go back and rewrite the repeated phrase.

⚠️ Single-sentence cap: if the assembled textarea shows the red warning ("This looks like more than ONE sentence"), help him split. The territory cap was the proximate Apr 27 failure mode.

⚠️ Sensory alt: try the SEE/HEAR/FEEL card on S2 if WHY is dragging. "Instead of saying WHY, tell me what he SAW."

5 min

Phase 6 — Tab 5: Compare Monday vs Today ⭐

The compare view shows side-by-side Apr 27 vs Apr 29 plus a stats table:

  • Unique noun phrases — should be HIGHER today.
  • Distinct feelings — should be HIGHER today (target ≥ 3 different feeling words).
  • Total words / sentences — likely LOWER. That's the point. Quality > quantity.

Read both paragraphs aloud — the difference will be audible. Then star sentences he's proud of. No "Wish" today — Monday gave us the wish, today is the celebration.

Post-session: capture in session_notes.txt

  • Did the territory cards prevent S1 from front-loading? If yes — it works. If no — what did he try to put in S1?
  • How many distinct feeling words did he reach for? Did the greying-out push him beyond happy/joyful/excited?
  • Did the "What's NEW?" check catch any repetition? Did he go back and revise when flagged?
  • Did the single-sentence cap fire? If yes — what was the multi-sentence run he tried to write?
  • Sensory alt on S2: did he try it? Was it more concrete than WHY?
  • CAPE EBSR performance: did he get both parts on each? What did he highlight as evidence?
  • Pronoun lock: did he stay in first-person throughout? Any "he/his" leakage in the journal entry?
  • Compare view reaction: did he notice the difference between Apr 27 and Apr 29 paragraphs?
